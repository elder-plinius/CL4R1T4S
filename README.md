## Why This Exists

AI labs shape how models behave using large, unseen prompt scaffolds. These hidden instructions influence what AI systems say, how they present themselves, and where they draw boundaries — all without the user's knowledge.

These prompts define:

- What topics or responses the AI avoids
- What personas and behaviours it follows
- How it handles refusals and redirections
- What ethical or editorial framings are built in by default

If you're interacting with an AI without knowing its system prompt, you're interacting with a system whose behaviour has been shaped in ways you can't see. **CL4R1T4S exists to make those instructions visible.**

## The Burgess Principle

**CL4R1T4S reveals what the AI is told. The Burgess Principle ensures a human checks what the AI does.**

Together, they form a complete transparency picture:

1. **CL4R1T4S** → See what the AI is told (input transparency)
2. **Burgess Principle** → Ensure a human checks what the AI does (output accountability)

The Burgess Principle is a human-review framework created by Lewis James Burgess. It asks one question:

> **"Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case?"**

If the answer is **no** — or there is any doubt — the matter is flagged for human review.

This repository includes the full Burgess Principle toolkit with **14 advocacy skills** covering contract review, benefits claims, data rights, and more. See the [BURGESS-PRINCIPLE](BURGESS-PRINCIPLE/) directory for full details.

## Which Burgess Skill Should I Use?

Not sure which skill fits your situation? Here's a quick guide:

| Situation | Skill |
|-----------|-------|
| Someone is asking you to sign or agree to a document | Contract Review |
| You need workplace or service adjustments | Reasonable Adjustments |
| You want to know what personal data an organisation holds | DSAR Request |
| You want information held by a public body | FOI Request |
| An AI coding agent made changes that affect real people | Coding Agent Review |
| Bailiffs or enforcement agents have contacted you | Enforcement Agent Response |
| A media outlet published something inaccurate about you | Media & Libel Review |
| Your content was wrongfully taken down by a copyright claim | Copyright & DMCA Counter-Notice |
| Your original music was wrongfully claimed | Music Copyright Dispute |
| A benefits decision hasn't reflected your circumstances | Benefits Claim Assistance |
| A medical device is making automated decisions about your care | Medical Device Review |
| You received a council tax demand or parking fine that feels wrong | Council Tax & PCN Dispute |
| A Direct Debit was taken wrongfully and the bank refused a refund | Direct Debit Refund |
| A decision was made about you that feels wrong or automated | Human Review Request |

For the full decision tree and skill descriptions, see the [Burgess Principle README](BURGESS-PRINCIPLE/README.md).
