# Media & Libel Review — Burgess-Enhanced Skill

A calm, guided skill for responding to inaccurate, unfair, or repeatedly negative media coverage. Grounded in the Burgess Principle, it helps you draft a clear, polite letter to editors and journalists requesting confirmation that a real person reviewed the specific facts of your case before publishing.

Designed for people who feel misrepresented by the press and want to respond without aggression or excessive energy.

---

## The Burgess Principle

> **"Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case?"**

If the answer is no — or there is any doubt — the response flags the need for individual human consideration. See the human first.

---

## How It Works

1. **Describe the coverage** — which outlet, the headline, date, and what is inaccurate or unfair.
2. The skill helps you list the **specific facts** that were omitted or misrepresented.
3. It generates a **polite, structured letter** that:
   - Identifies the specific article by title, date, and link
   - Asks the outlet to confirm whether a human editor reviewed the specific facts of your case
   - Lists the facts that need correction
   - Requests a response and any corrections or right-of-reply
4. Any point where your specific facts need individual review is **clearly flagged**.
5. You receive the draft ready to **copy, adjust, and send**.

---

## Example Prompts

| You say | What happens |
|---------|--------------|
| *"A newspaper wrote about me and got key facts wrong"* | Generates a polite letter requesting human review of the specific facts and corrections. |
| *"I want to challenge inaccurate press coverage about my business"* | Creates a structured response identifying the inaccuracies and requesting editorial review. |
| *"How do I request a right of reply from a media outlet?"* | Explains the process and helps draft a calm, factual request. |
| *"An article keeps being shared that misrepresents what happened"* | Drafts a response asking for corrections and confirmation of human editorial review. |

---

## When to Use This Skill

- A news outlet or journalist published something inaccurate about you
- Key facts were omitted or misrepresented in media coverage
- You want to request corrections or a right-of-reply
- You suspect coverage was based on assumptions or narrative templates rather than individual review
- You want a written record that you raised factual concerns

---

## Important Notes

- **This is not legal advice.** It helps you prepare a clear, respectful response.
- Designed for people with limited energy — calm pace, no jargon, no pressure.
- Every flagged point is an invitation to pause and involve a real person who understands your situation.
- You always have the final say on tone and content before sending anything.
