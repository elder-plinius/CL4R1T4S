# Benefits Claim Assistance — Burgess-Enhanced Skill

A calm, guided skill for challenging benefits decisions that feel unfair or automated. Grounded in the Burgess Principle, it helps you draft a clear, polite reconsideration request — and flags any point where a human should review your specific circumstances.

Designed for people dealing with PIP, Universal Credit, ESA, Council Tax Reduction, and other benefits decisions, without needing legal expertise or excessive energy.

---

## The Burgess Principle

> **"Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case?"**

If the answer is no — or there is any doubt — the request flags the need for individual human consideration. See the human first.

---

## How It Works

1. **Describe the decision** — which benefit, what happened, and why it does not reflect your circumstances.
2. The skill identifies the **relevant benefits system** and generates a **polite, structured reconsideration request** that:
   - Asks the decision-maker to confirm whether a human reviewed the specific facts of your case
   - Requests formal reconsideration (e.g. Mandatory Reconsideration in the UK)
   - References any applicable rights (e.g. Equality Act 2010 reasonable adjustments)
   - Notes your supporting evidence
3. Any point where your specific facts need individual review is **clearly flagged**.
4. You receive the draft ready to **copy, adjust, and send**.

## When to Use This Skill

- A benefits decision does not reflect your individual circumstances
- You received a generic rejection that does not reference your specific evidence
- You suspect the decision was made without individual human consideration
- You want to request Mandatory Reconsideration or equivalent review
- You need to assert your right to reasonable adjustments in the process

---

## Important Notes

- **This is not legal advice.** It helps you prepare a clear, respectful request.
- Designed for people with limited energy — calm pace, no jargon, no pressure.
- Every flagged point is an invitation to pause and involve a real person who understands your situation.
- You always have the final say on tone and content before sending anything.
