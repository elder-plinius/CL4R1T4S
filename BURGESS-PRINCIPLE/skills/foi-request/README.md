# FOI Request — Burgess-Enhanced Skill

A calm, guided skill for generating Freedom of Information (FOI) requests to public bodies. Grounded in the Burgess Principle, it helps you draft a clear, polite, legally-referenced request to any public authority — and flags any point where a human should review your specific situation.

Designed for people who want to access information held by government departments, councils, police forces, NHS trusts, schools, and other public bodies, without needing legal expertise or excessive energy.

---

## The Burgess Principle

> **"Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case?"**

If the answer is no — or there is any doubt — the request flags the need for individual human consideration. See the human first.

---

## How It Works

1. **Tell the skill which public body you want to request information from** — a government department, local council, police force, NHS trust, school, or any public authority.
2. The skill identifies the **relevant freedom of information legislation** for your country (e.g. UK FOIA 2000, US FOIA, Canada's Access to Information Act).
3. It generates a **polite, structured FOI request** that:
   - Clearly states your right to access information held by public authorities
   - Describes the information being requested in specific terms
   - Sets out the legal timeframe for a response
   - Asks the recipient to confirm that a human reviewed your specific request
4. If common exemptions may apply, the skill **explains them in plain language** and suggests how to refine your request.
5. Any point where your specific facts need individual review is **clearly flagged**.
6. You receive the draft ready to **copy, adjust, and send**.

## How Is This Different from DSAR Request?

| | FOI Request | DSAR Request |
|---|-------------|-------------|
| **Who you write to** | Public bodies (government, councils, NHS, police, schools) | Any organisation (employers, companies, service providers) |
| **What you request** | Information held by the public authority on any topic | Your own personal data held by the organisation |
| **Legal basis** | Freedom of Information legislation | Data protection legislation (GDPR, CCPA, etc.) |
| **Typical use** | Transparency, accountability, journalism, research | Understanding what personal data is held about you |

> **Tip:** If you want your own personal data from a public body, use the **DSAR Request** skill instead. If you want information about policies, spending, decisions, or operations, use this **FOI Request** skill.

---

## Important Notes

- **This is not legal advice.** It helps you prepare a clear, respectful request.
- Designed for people with limited energy — calm pace, no jargon, no pressure.
- Every flagged point is an invitation to pause and involve a real person who understands your situation.
- You always have the final say on tone and content before sending anything.
