# Medical Device Review — Burgess-Enhanced Skill

A calm, guided skill for requesting meaningful human clinician review of algorithmic decisions in medical devices. Grounded in the Burgess Principle, it helps you draft a clear, polite letter to device manufacturers, clinicians, or regulatory bodies — ensuring that automated adjustments to your medical device have been reviewed against your individual circumstances.

Designed for people with hearing aids, pacemakers, insulin pumps, continuous glucose monitors, or other algorithmic medical devices who want their specific clinical profile reviewed by a real person.

---

## The Burgess Principle

> **"Was a human clinician or qualified member of the clinical team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case?"**

If the answer is no — or there is any doubt — the request flags the need for individual clinical review. See the human first.

---

## How It Works

1. **Describe your device and concern** — the type of device, your condition(s), and what concerns you about its automated decisions.
2. The skill helps you articulate your **individual clinical context** — how your profile differs from population averages.
3. It generates a **polite, structured letter** that:
   - Identifies the specific device by type, model, and reference
   - Asks the clinical team to confirm whether a human clinician reviewed the specific facts of your case
   - Requests that the review covers your exact profile, comorbidities, reasonable adjustments, and device logs
   - Requests a written summary documented in your clinical notes
4. Any point where your specific facts need clinical review is **clearly flagged**.
5. You receive the draft ready to **copy, adjust, and send**.

## Example Prompts

| You say | What happens |
|---------|--------------|
| *"My hearing aid keeps switching modes and I don't think it's right for me"* | Generates a letter requesting clinician review of the algorithmic settings against your specific hearing profile. |
| *"I want to check if my insulin pump settings were reviewed by a real person"* | Creates a request for confirmation of human clinical review of your pump's algorithmic decisions. |
| *"My pacemaker rate response doesn't feel right — how do I ask for a review?"* | Drafts a polite request for a clinician to review the device's automated adjustments against your cardiac profile. |
| *"Who do I write to about my medical device's automated decisions?"* | Explains the process and helps draft a request to the appropriate clinical team. |

## Device Types Covered

| Device type | Example concerns |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Hearing aids** | AutoSense mode switching, gain changes, environment classification |
| **Pacemakers** | Rate response, therapy delivery, remote monitoring alerts |
| **Insulin pumps** | Basal rate adjustments, bolus calculations, sensor-pump loops |
| **Continuous glucose monitors** | Alert thresholds, predictive algorithms, calibration |
| **Remote monitoring systems** | Automated triage, alert prioritisation, escalation rules |

---

## Important Notes

- **This is not medical or legal advice.** It helps you prepare for meaningful clinical review.
- Designed for people with limited energy — calm pace, no jargon, no pressure.
- Every flagged point is an invitation to pause and involve a real clinician who understands your individual case.
- You always have the final say on tone and content before sending anything.
