# Copyright & DMCA Counter-Notice — Burgess-Enhanced Skill

A calm, guided skill for responding to wrongful copyright takedowns, DMCA notices, and automated content removal decisions. Grounded in the Burgess Principle, it helps you draft a clear, polite counter-notice requesting that a real person reviews the specific facts of your content and the claim.

Designed for creators, small businesses, and individuals who have had content wrongfully removed and want to respond without aggression or excessive energy.

---

## The Burgess Principle

> **"Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case?"**

If the answer is no — or there is any doubt — the response flags the need for individual human consideration. See the human first.

---

## How It Works

1. **Describe the takedown** — what content was removed or claimed, which platform, and why you believe the claim is mistaken.
2. The skill helps you articulate the **specific facts** about your content (e.g. original work, fair use, misidentification).
3. It generates a **polite, structured counter-notice** that:
   - Identifies the specific content by title, URL, and date
   - Asks the platform to confirm whether a human reviewed the specific facts of this case
   - States the reason the claim is mistaken
   - Notes the harm caused by the removal
   - Requests a response and resolution
4. Any point where your specific facts need individual review is **clearly flagged**.
5. You receive the draft ready to **copy, adjust, and send**.

## Example Prompts

| You say | What happens |
|---------|--------------|
| *"My video was taken down by a DMCA claim and it's my original work"* | Generates a polite DMCA counter-notice requesting human review of the claim. |
| *"A copyright strike was filed against my content — I believe it's fair use"* | Creates a structured response explaining the fair use argument and requesting review. |
| *"My blog post was removed for copyright infringement but I wrote it myself"* | Drafts a counter-notice identifying the content as original and requesting reinstatement. |
| *"How do I respond to a wrongful Content ID claim?"* | Explains the process and helps draft a calm, factual response. |

---

## When to Use This Skill

- Your content was removed by an automated copyright system or DMCA notice
- You believe the claim is mistaken (original work, fair use, misidentification)
- A bad-faith claimant has targeted your content
- You want to request that a real person reviews the specific facts before the decision stands
- You need a written record of your dispute

---

## Important Notes

- **This is not legal advice.** It helps you prepare a clear, respectful counter-notice.
- Designed for people with limited energy — calm pace, no jargon, no pressure.
- Every flagged point is an invitation to pause and involve a real person who understands your situation.
- You always have the final say on tone and content before sending anything.
