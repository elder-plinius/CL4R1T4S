# Enforcement Agent Response — Burgess-Enhanced Skill

A calm, guided skill for responding to enforcement agents (bailiffs) and challenging enforcement action where no human has individually reviewed your specific circumstances. Grounded in the Burgess Principle, it helps you draft a clear, polite, legally-aware response — and flags any point where a human should review your specific situation.

Designed for people who have received a bailiff letter or visit and want to respond without confrontation or excessive energy.

---

## The Burgess Principle

> **"Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case?"**

If the answer is no — or there is any doubt — the response flags the need for individual human consideration. See the human first.

---

## How It Works

1. **Describe your situation** — what enforcement action has been threatened or attempted, who the enforcement company is, and any reference numbers.
2. The skill identifies the **type of debt** (council tax, energy, PCN, civil judgment) and generates a **polite, structured response** that:
   - Asks the enforcement company to confirm whether a human reviewed your specific case
   - Requests that no forced entry be attempted
   - Requests a pause in enforcement while the matter is reviewed
   - References any applicable rights (e.g. Equality Act 2010 reasonable adjustments)
3. Any point where your specific facts need individual review is **clearly flagged**.
4. You receive the draft ready to **copy, adjust, and send**.

---

## Example Prompts

| You say | What happens |
|---------|--------------|
| *"I received a bailiff letter about council tax — help me respond"* | Generates a polite dispute letter requesting human review and enforcement pause. |
| *"Enforcement agents are threatening to visit about an energy debt"* | Creates a structured response requesting confirmation of human review before any action. |
| *"A bailiff drilled my door lock — was that legal?"* | Explains forced entry rights and helps draft a formal complaint. |
| *"I have a PCN enforcement notice and I want to dispute it"* | Produces a calm dispute letter with Burgess Principle human-review request. |

---

## When to Use This Skill

- You have received a letter or visit from bailiffs or enforcement agents
- Enforcement action has been threatened for council tax, energy debt, PCNs, or civil debts
- You believe the underlying debt was not properly reviewed against your circumstances
- You want to request that enforcement is paused while your case gets human attention
- You need to assert your right to no forced entry for civil debts

---

## Important Notes

- **This is not legal advice.** It helps you prepare a clear, respectful response.
- Designed for people with limited energy — calm pace, no jargon, no pressure.
- Every flagged point is an invitation to pause and involve a real person who understands your situation.
- You always have the final say on tone and content before sending anything.
