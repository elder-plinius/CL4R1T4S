# Council Tax & PCN Dispute — Burgess-Enhanced Skill

A calm, guided skill for challenging council tax demands, parking fines (PCNs), and other local authority charges that feel unfair or automated. Grounded in the Burgess Principle, it helps you draft a clear, polite dispute letter — and flags any point where a human should review your specific circumstances.

Designed for people dealing with local authority demands who want to respond without confrontation or excessive energy.

---

## The Burgess Principle

> **"Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case?"**

If the answer is no — or there is any doubt — the dispute flags the need for individual human consideration. See the human first.

---

## How It Works

1. **Describe the charge** — council tax demand, PCN, or other local authority charge, with any reference numbers.
2. The skill helps you identify the **specific issues** — incorrect amounts, wrong dates, no individual consideration.
3. It generates a **polite, structured dispute letter** that:
   - Asks the council to confirm whether a human reviewed the specific facts of your case
   - Requests a full breakdown of the amount and dates
   - Requests that enforcement is paused while the matter is reviewed
   - References any applicable rights (e.g. Equality Act 2010 reasonable adjustments)
4. Any point where your specific facts need individual review is **clearly flagged**.
5. You receive the draft ready to **copy, adjust, and send**.

## Example Prompts

| You say | What happens |
|---------|--------------|
| *"I received a council tax bill that doesn't match my circumstances"* | Generates a formal dispute letter requesting human review and a breakdown of the charges. |
| *"I want to challenge a parking fine — I don't think anyone looked at the details"* | Creates a structured PCN challenge with Burgess Principle human-review request. |
| *"The council sent a demand addressed to 'the occupier' — that's not right"* | Drafts a dispute letter noting the impersonal correspondence and requesting individual review. |
| *"How do I dispute a council tax liability order?"* | Explains the process and helps draft a calm, factual dispute. |

---

## When to Use This Skill

- You received a council tax demand that does not reflect your circumstances
- A parking fine (PCN) was issued without individual consideration
- Correspondence was addressed to "the occupier" rather than you personally
- You believe the amount or dates are incorrect
- You want enforcement paused while your case gets proper human review

---

## Important Notes

- **This is not legal advice.** It helps you prepare a clear, respectful dispute.
- Designed for people with limited energy — calm pace, no jargon, no pressure.
- Every flagged point is an invitation to pause and involve a real person who understands your situation.
- You always have the final say on tone and content before sending anything.
