# Design Principles for Burgess-Enhanced Skills

Use these principles when creating, reviewing, or improving Burgess-enhanced advocacy skills.

---

## Core Principles (Burgess-Aligned)

1. **See the human first.** Every skill must include a clear checkpoint where the user is reminded that a real person should review the specific facts of their specific case.

2. **Calm and respectful.** No aggressive language, no pressure, no rush. The tone should feel like sitting beside a friend and working through something together.

3. **Minimalist.** Zero bloat. One purpose per skill. No unnecessary features, no feature creep.

4. **Zero-energy.** Designed for people with limited energy or hidden disabilities. Short prompts, clear outputs, no cognitive overload.

5. **Privacy-first.** No data leaves the device unless explicitly required. No tracking, no analytics, no accounts.

6. **Plain language.** Avoid jargon. Explain legal concepts in everyday words. Bold key phrases sparingly for scannability.

7. **Never legal advice.** Every skill must end with a clear disclaimer. These are assistive tools, not substitutes for professional guidance.

---

## Accessibility-First Design

- Prioritise high contrast and readable typography.
- Ensure keyboard navigation and screen-reader compatibility where applicable.
- Use calm, respectful, minimalist layouts — avoid overwhelming motion or dense information.
- Always include clear "human review" cues in generated outputs.

---

## Recommended Design References

- **Apple Human Interface Guidelines** — clean, accessible, calm interfaces.
- **Linear** — minimal, focused, excellent typography and spacing.
- **GitHub Primer** — clear hierarchy, accessible components.

---

## The Burgess Binary

Every Burgess-enhanced skill should apply this binary question at appropriate points:

> **"Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts and implications of this for my individual situation?"**

If the answer is **no** — or there is any doubt — the matter is flagged for human review.

This is not a checklist item. It is the foundational principle that every skill is built on.
