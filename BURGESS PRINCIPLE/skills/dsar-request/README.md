# DSAR Request — Burgess-Enhanced Skill

A calm, guided skill for generating Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs). Grounded in the Burgess Principle, it helps you draft a clear, polite, legally-referenced request to any organisation that holds your personal data — and flags any point where a human should review your specific situation.

Designed for people who want to understand what data is held about them, without needing legal expertise or excessive energy.

---

## The Burgess Principle

> **"Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case?"**

If the answer is no — or there is any doubt — the request flags the need for individual human consideration. See the human first.

---

## How It Works

1. **Tell the skill who you want to send a DSAR to** — an employer, a company, a service provider, or any organisation that holds your data.
2. The skill identifies the **relevant data protection legislation** for your country (e.g. UK GDPR, EU GDPR, CCPA).
3. It generates a **polite, structured DSAR** that:
   - Clearly states your right to access your personal data
   - Specifies the categories of data you are requesting
   - Sets out the legal timeframe for a response
   - Asks the recipient to confirm that a human reviewed your specific request
4. Any point where your specific facts need individual review is **clearly flagged**.
5. You receive the draft ready to **copy, adjust, and send**.

## Example Prompts

| You say | What happens |
|---------|--------------|
| *"I want to see what data my employer holds about me"* | Generates a DSAR letter referencing UK GDPR with a 30-day response deadline. |
| *"Send a data access request to my insurance company"* | Creates a clear, polite DSAR for the relevant jurisdiction. |
| *"What personal data can I request under GDPR?"* | Explains your rights and helps you decide what to ask for. |
| *"Draft a DSAR for a US company under CCPA"* | Produces a California Consumer Privacy Act request. |

---

## Important Notes

- **This is not legal advice.** It helps you prepare a clear, respectful request.
- Designed for people with limited energy — calm pace, no jargon, no pressure.
- Every flagged point is an invitation to pause and involve a real person who understands your situation.
- You always have the final say on tone and content before sending anything.
